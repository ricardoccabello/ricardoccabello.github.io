II Temporary retail environment

IKKS temporary space

Technical development for a temporary fashion space in San Sebastián de los Reyes, prepared for a short installation window inside an operating shopping centre.

Case study at a glance
Challenge
Resolve sales, fitting rooms, storage and point of sale before a short installation window inside an operating centre.
Role
Location plan, proposed layout, dimensioned drawings, elevations and technical documentation.
Process
Define the relationships between the main elements early, then make every dimension and interface easy to read.
Outcome
A legible technical package prepared for production and handover to the person responsible for installation.
What I learned
Temporary spaces need information that is faster to understand, not documentation with less precision.
I

The challenge

Sales, fitting rooms, storage and point of sale had to fit into a temporary footprint and work clearly from opening day. With little time available on site, the project information needed to resolve dimensions and relationships before installation began.

II

My contribution

I prepared the location plan, proposed layout, dimensioned drawings and elevations for the main elements, including the checkout counter and fitting room partitions. My responsibility was to leave a complete, legible technical package ready for production and handover to the installation lead.

III

What stayed with me

Temporary spaces do not need less precision. They need information that is faster to read, because there is very little time to clarify a missing dimension once the installation has started.
