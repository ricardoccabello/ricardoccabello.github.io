I Survey

What a useful retail survey needs to communicate

A useful survey records more than dimensions. Before measuring, I try to understand circulation, fixed elements, existing furniture and the points where a future layout is most likely to meet a constraint.

Photographs need an order. Wide views explain the space; closer images record junctions, services, finishes and details that may affect drawings, procurement or production.

The test is simple: someone who was not present should be able to understand the site well enough to continue the project without guessing what the missing information might be.
