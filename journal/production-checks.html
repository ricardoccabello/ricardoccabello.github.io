IV Production

Five checks before a project goes to production

First, dimensions: overall sizes, clearances, material thicknesses and every point where the new work meets the existing space.

Second, materials: descriptions, references and finishes must be clear enough for procurement and the workshop to understand the same specification.

Third, assembly logic: how pieces are divided, transported and joined, and whether the proposed sequence makes sense in the available space.

Fourth, responsibility: every pending decision needs a named person or team. Unassigned details are the easiest ones to overlook.

Finally, consistency: plans, elevations, schedules and written notes should describe one project, not four slightly different versions of it.
